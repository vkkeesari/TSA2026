Frequently Asked Questions

1. How do I choose between Weddington and Waxhaw resources in one day?

Start your day in Weddington by visiting outdoor parks, school-affiliated programs, or quieter community spaces that are typically more active in the morning. These locations are ideal for recreation, educational activities, or early events. In the afternoon, transition to Waxhaw, where the downtown area becomes more active with local shops, cultural sites, and community events. This approach allows you to experience both areas efficiently without overlapping schedules or missing peak activity times.

2. Where do I submit a community drive request?

You can submit a community drive request through the Community Pulse page on the hub by completing the official drive request form. Once submitted, your request is routed through verified community partners who review, coordinate, and help support your initiative. The process also includes optional planning assistance, such as guidance on logistics, promotion, and connecting with local volunteers.

3. How can I verify organization details before visiting?

To ensure accuracy, first cross-check the organization's information on the Reference Page within the hub. Then, visit the organization's official website directly to confirm details such as hours, location, and current services. This two-step verification process helps prevent outdated or incorrect information and ensures you have the most reliable details before planning your visit.

4. Can students volunteer and earn service documentation?

Yes, students can participate in volunteer opportunities through programs like Community Pulse and Senior Connection. After completing activities, students can submit their participation through verified forms or partner organizations. These submissions can then be used as official documentation for service hours required by schools, clubs, or academic programs.

5. What are the best links for same-day local planning?

For up-to-date information on events, schedules, and community updates, use trusted local websites such as official town pages, library guides, and community calendars. These sources provide real-time updates on closures, event changes, and new activities, making them the most reliable tools for planning your day on short notice.

6. Where can I find the full 2026 meetings and event calendar?

Navigate to the Local Navigator page and scroll to Section 3, where all events, town meetings, and closures for both Waxhaw and Weddington are organized in chronological order. This section is designed to give users a complete, easy-to-follow timeline for the year, helping with both short-term and long-term planning.

7. Which page should I use for urgent food or blood drive needs?

Use the Community Pulse page to find urgent needs, including food drives, blood donations, and supply requests. This page features live updates, active need trackers, and verified drop-off locations. It is regularly updated to ensure users can quickly respond to time-sensitive community needs.

8. How do seniors request support and how do volunteers apply?

Visit the Senior Connection page, where both seniors and volunteers can access dedicated forms. Seniors can submit a Help Request form to receive assistance with services such as transportation, errands, or companionship. Volunteers can apply through the Volunteer form, which connects them with available opportunities. All submissions are processed securely and routed directly to organizers for follow-up.

9. Where do I report a new organization that should be added to the hub?

To suggest a new organization, use the resource suggestion form located on the main hub page. Provide key details such as the organization's name, category, contact information, and a brief explanation of its value to the community. Submissions are reviewed before being added to ensure accuracy and relevance.

10. Can I access FAQ directly from the hub homepage?

Yes, the FAQ page is accessible directly from the hub homepage through the main navigation bar. It follows the same consistent navigation structure used across the Hub and Local Navigator pages, making it easy to locate and access from any part of the site.
